Timeline
- 1913: second federal income tax (16th Amendment)
- 1920s: treasury officials exclude group insurance from income-tax base
- 1942: wage control act limited wage growth for inflation reasons but excluded healthcare benefits from being counted as wages (effectively endorsing this)
- 1953: wage ceilings off
- 1954: congress explicitly codifies tax exclusion for businesses (TODO: check that this is right wording)
- 2010s: ACA
  - Bans, among other things, individual underwriting for individual market.
  - Requires everyone to have coverage or face a penalty. Great for pre-existing conditions/unhealthy people, bad for healthy people.
  - 2019: excise tax to fund ACA with high-cost employer sponsored plans failed twice: [Cadillac tax](https://www.npr.org/sections/health-shots/2019/08/14/750859901/cadillac-tax-on-generous-health-plans-may-be-headed-to-congressional-junkyard)
- 2017: American Health Care Act (AHCA) fails in Senate
- 2017: Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA): starting 2019, federal penalty reduced to 0 for not having coverage; some states still have it (such as CA)
- 2021: American Rescue Plan Act (ARPA): capped ACA premiums at 8.5% income; expiring end of 2022
- 2022: Inflation Reduction Act (IRA): extended cap on ACA premiums to end of 2025

## "Fixes" with tech

- Forward (2016-2024): tech-enabled direct primary care business based on a cash-pay model that doesn't take insurance
- Amperos (2024-): seed 2024; doing somewhere between 5-10M in revenue as of late 2025. AI phone calls for denials/collections/prior auth (plus some browser stuff) for providers. This definitely leads to an arms race bc insurance on the other side is billed per-case, so now on top of their existing adversarialness, they also have anti-AI measures against inefficient calls where they will just hang up if they detect AI.
- Tandem (2024-): 37M series A as of June 2025. They make money off pharma contracts so that they can provide their main product for free (prior auth for pharmacies). Notably they don't do much marketing and claim to do a lot of sales through word of mouth. They made a big deal about "uplift" from better adherence to drugs using their improved prior auth rates and data-gathering (?). They have a TON of ops folks.
- 2024: United Healthcare's Gold Card program starts to build trust-based prior auth system https://www.uhc.com/news-articles/newsroom/gold-card - seemingly admirable, but don't they have an incentive to increase prior auths?
- [The Lanby (2021-)](https://www.thelanby.com/), a direct primary care subscription service

### Less important/tangential

- Tennr (2023-): 101M series C ~June 2025. Automate patient referrals (i.e. PCP -> specialist) which is usually a fax
